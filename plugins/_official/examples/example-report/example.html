Executive summary

The team is ready for a narrow first deployment.

Support can safely introduce AI assistance in triage, draft replies, and knowledge-base routing. Full automation should wait until quality review and privacy controls are proven on real tickets.

Best first use

AI should prepare drafts and summarize context, while human agents keep final-send authority.

Main constraint

The knowledge base is uneven. Billing and integration articles need cleanup before automation expands.

Decision needed

Approve a 45-day pilot with weekly QA sampling and a privacy review before broader rollout.

01 / Context

Ticket volume is growing faster than headcount.

The support queue has increased 31% over the last two quarters, while staffed hours increased 12%. The gap is concentrated in repeat setup questions, account-management tasks, and tickets that require a handoff between support and solutions engineering.

The goal is not to remove human judgment. The practical opportunity is to reduce context-gathering and first-draft time so senior agents spend more attention on ambiguous or high-risk cases.

02 / Findings

Three workstreams have enough structure for a pilot.

Triage, draft response, and article recommendation have clear inputs, observable outcomes, and low downside when humans approve the final answer. Refunds, enterprise security questions, and contract exceptions should remain outside the pilot.

  • Triage labels already match the routing model used by team leads, reducing retraining work.
  • Drafts save time only when the linked source article is current and specific.
  • Escalation detection must be measured separately from response speed.

05 / Recommendation

Approve a controlled pilot with a hard quality gate.

Launch with 12 agents, two queues, and final-send human approval. Continue only if escalation accuracy stays at or above the current baseline and customer satisfaction does not fall by more than one point during the pilot window.

Appendix

Methodology and assumptions.

  • Queue review sampled 1,200 tickets across setup, billing, integrations, and account operations.
  • Handling-time estimate assumes draft generation saves 3-5 minutes on eligible tickets after week two.
  • Privacy review must confirm prompt logging, data retention, and customer-data redaction before launch.
